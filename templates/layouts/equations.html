SIR Differential Equations

$${dS \over dt} = {-\beta SI}$$

$${dI \over dt} = {\beta SI - \gamma I}$$

$${dR \over dt} = {\gamma I}$$

The three primary equations used to calculate the SIR models. S is the population number that are currently susceptible to infection. I is the count of population that are currently infectious (symptoms or no symptoms). R is the population count that are removed from infectiousness and no longer susceptible. So those that have recovered...or not.
